Books in my Bookshelf

A Life in Administration

This book, 'Life in Administration,' is an autobiographical translation of Ghazi Al-Gosaibi's work. It covers his life journey starting from his early stages until his appointment as an ambassador to London in 1992. It is considered one of his most prominent works. The author narrates his experiences and his approach to managing the positions he held. He shares some anecdotes, humorous incidents, and obstacles that he encountered. He also extracts some guidance and advice for administrators in general, and specifically for young individuals.

The Usfuriyah

The Usfuriyah is a novel written by the Saudi author Ghazi Abdul Rahman Al Gosaibi. It spans approximately three hundred pages and was ranked 35th on the list of the top 100 Arabic novels. The events take place in 'The Usfuriyah' (the psychiatric hospital), where the protagonist, a professor, is one of the patients. The story revolves around therapy sessions and conversations with the psychiatrist, which can be seen as a long and satirical dialogue. It touches upon various topics and leaves no aspect of both the Arab and Western world unmentioned. The novel is rich in ideas and diverse information about prominent events and well-known figures, whether political, intellectual, or scientific. It serves as a knowledgeable and cultural encyclopedia, providing quick and varied insights into the lives of individuals you may have heard of but never had the chance to truly know.
